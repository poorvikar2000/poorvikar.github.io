As part of an overarching effort to offer genderfluid kidswear to their customers, Marks and Spencer provided an industry brief to MA students at LCF with the aim of widening their audience and attracting younger customers.


The marketing strategy for the gender soft kids wear range was developed using the SOSTAC model (Smith, 2020) as its various stages ensure holistic planning, execution, and impact measurement of marketing activities.


A situation analysis revealed that M&S’s overall communications goals are aligned with current market opportunities with reference to both consumer and competitor contexts. The brand has a strong foundation inn social media and offline engagement. However, they need to be leveraged for further growth in order to stand out in a highly competitive market with fast evolving consumer preferences. These findings can be applied to the specific marketing activities for the GenderFluid collection to foster conversation and garner attention.


The overarching vision of the marketing strategy for the GenderSoft collection is to strengthen the perceived identity of M&S as not only traditional but also adaptable, broadening their target demographic and establishing relevance among Millennial and Generation Z parents. To further define this vision, the RACE model (Chaffey, 2022) was used to chart out aims and corresponding SMART objectives (Doran, 1981).

Segmentation, Targeting and Positioning was used to achieve targeted marketing.


Younger Millennial parents and in some cases Generation Z parents are the primary segment whereas retaining Generation X consumers is the secondary target. A digital first strategy highlighting sustainability, gender softness and convenience is appropriate for such an audience that prioritises family values and modernity.


For the positioning stage, a competitor positioning map was created with the comparative variables being Sustainability and Gender Fluid clothing variety. These variables were chosen within the scope of this project highlighting the differentiating factors among these competitors. With similar price points and competitive offerings, it can be difficult to carve out a unique identity for a brand (Prahalad and Ramaswamy, 2004).


Working towards marketing sustainability and progressive concepts, M&S can differentiate itself from its competitors and position itself as a traditional yet adaptable brand that takes care of the evolving needs of customers.

A Marketing Mix and Content Strategy were developed for the execution of the repositioning efforts


Given the audience expansion goal of the project brief, it is important to leverage all channels reaching two different segments – older and younger parents. These specialised and targeted campaigns (Figure 48) will run alongside regular marketing activities such as email marketing, search engine optimisation, print ads etc.


Research indicates that interactions affecting the “cognitive” aspect of customers, by encouraging constant attention and participation across channels can improve brand loyalty (Brun et al., 2017). Events like the pre-launch party, weekly sessions and mural hunts will create memorable family bonding experiences and encourage kids to express their creativity and think out of the box. By focusing on digital channels, these campaigns will reach Millennial and in some cases Generation Z parents resulting in increased awareness about the evolved brand image.



The collection launch will be separated into four biweekly drops ensuring continued freshness throughout the marketing period. A buzz will be initiated in August before the launch in September. Following this, majority of the marketing activities will take place up until mid-November. Reporting and statistics will be drawn at the end of November to gauge success.




Diversification activities i.e., offering new products (gender-soft kid swear) to new customers (Millennial and Generation Z parents) is highly risky due to an unfamiliar environment (Ansoff, 1957). Therefore, measuring results are critical for this campaign for future improvement.The KPI targets have been set to benchmark success. Against a market standard ROI of 5:1 which is considered profitable (Adobe, 2022), the target was set at 7:1. Given that the brief is aiming for a changed brand perception, a higher ROI target has been set to indicate a greater impact of the campaign. To achieve this, the collection would have to generate a direct revenue of £4.9 million from marketing activities. This is an achievable target as our WSSI forecasts total sales to be £17 million. The digital ROI has been set higher given its larger budget allocation and higher importance for this campaign. Finally, in-store and e-commerce targets have been set to indicate improved sales and stronger brand loyalty.
