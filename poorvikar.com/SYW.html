Save Your Wardrobe is a digital wardrobe management and aftercare services platform promoting circularity in fashion and extending the lifespan of existing wardrobes. The company is incorporating Digital Product Passports into their platforms and proposed an industry brief to MA students at UAL for finding creative pathways to disseminate the technology among users.


An in-depth market trend analysis in terms of technology and brand strategies revealed  the emergence of new business models that unify the physical and digital aspects of the fashion into phy-gital experiences.


SYW is at the forefront of developing new value exchange models prioritizing circularity (Save Your Wardrobe, 2019) using technology. Therefore, wide acceptance of such phenomena can be leveraged to trial emerging technologies like DPP.


Another Trend identified was “next-gen personalisation” identified on WGSN (Housley et al., 2021). This avenue aims to discourage consumers from impulse buying by offering personalized options. Currently, SYW offers personalisation by creating individual digital wardrobes and providing AI powered outfit prompts (Benard, 2020).

There are many applications available in the market offering a similar core service as SYW, i.e. a wardrobe digitisation platform.  Competitive analysis was conducted to compare these applications to identify the SYW’s positioning against its competitors and explore re-positioning possibilities using Strategic Group Mapping.


A key insight was the common use of AI for wardrobe management. However, there were only two applications with sustainability and mindfulness at their core and just one other application with a circular business model.


Therefore, while AI and wardrobe management is a saturated market with large players, there is a gap in the market for the use of blockchain in a sustainability and circularity context. By leveraging the use of DPP, SYW can reposition itself. Secondly, by strengthening its brand image of sustainability and building on a circular business model in line with currently prevailing macro trends, SYW can be a unique option in an increasingly saturated market.

According to the SYW Brief (Kourda, 2023), the majority of users are UK based and belong to the Generation Z and Millennial categories consisting of 80% female users and 20% male users.


Primary research presented in-depth information about the usability of the application. Based on the Radical and fast-Moving innovation context (Aas and Breunig, 2017), samples included both new users to understand growth opportunities and familiar users to delve into the user-experience. Analysis revealed the consumers are fairly aware about Digital Passports and are willing to learn more.


It was found that while visualizations are important to users, they must have functionality. While moving towards a slow fashion lifestyle, they appreciate wardrobe planning features and insights and attempt to engage in mindful shopping. Consumers would also like to see marketplace based use cases as well digital immersion on the app that centralizes multiple aspects of slow fashion into a personalized experience.


SYW can create a space with little to no competition by innovating applications and visualizations for DPP. This makes it relevant for the application of Kim and Maunorgne’s (2005) Blue Ocean theory which allows smaller players to differentiate without bearing high costs. The ERRC grid (Kim and Maunorgne, 2005) was adopted to define an ideal environment for such a strategy

The marketing strategy for disseminating the DPP technology and encouraging its adoption focuses on enhancing the user experience within the SYW app. A dedicated DPP section will be introduced with engaging features.


While shopping, users can easily scan a potential purchase's DPP code using the embedded scanner, revealing its compatibility with their wardrobe. Additionally, a mockup feature will display different compatible pieces from the wardrobe on a 3D avatar of the user, allowing for virtual outfit planning. The avatar will be created using photogrammetry, ensuring an accurate representation. Avatars can be used to display outfit ideas and so forth on social media and can even be teamed up with an AR representation feature. This links back to the target users being digital natives.


Finally, users can access their entire passport gallery, where individual passports are presented as interactive cards that open up upon selection. Each card will provide access to the raw passport data, offering transparency and detailed information. These cards can also be marketed as NFT collectibles linked to the physical items to create a sense of "FOMO" and encourage adoption.


This enhanced visualisation, blending technology and personalization, will drive the adoption of DPP within the SYW app and differentiate it in the market, leveraging digital marketing strategies to create awareness and attract users seeking sustainable and mindful fashion experiences.
